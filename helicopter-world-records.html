Helicopter World Records — The Fastest, Highest, and Most Daring Flights Ever

✍️ By HelipadUSA Staff📅 Updated June 2025⏱️ 8 min read

Helicopters have been pushing the limits of rotary wing aviation since Igor Sikorsky first lifted off in 1939. From blistering speed records to solo flights around the world in a light piston aircraft, the record books of helicopter aviation are filled with achievements that seem impossible until someone goes and does them. Here are the most remarkable helicopter world records ever set.

Speed Records

253 mph
Absolute Speed Record — FAI Certified

Westland Lynx — August 11, 1986

The absolute helicopter speed record has stood for nearly four decades. On August 11, 1986, a specially modified Westland Lynx piloted by John Trevor Egginton reached 400.87 km/h over the Severn Estuary in England. The Lynx was fitted with uprated engines and specially designed BERP rotor blades. No conventional helicopter has come close to matching this record since.

Modern compound helicopters have exceeded this speed in test flights, but the FAI absolute record for a conventional helicopter still belongs to the Lynx. Understanding how fast these aircraft can go puts the typical 100 mph cruise of a Robinson R22 in interesting perspective.

293 mph
Compound Helicopter Speed — Unofficial

Sikorsky X2 — 2010

Sikorsky's experimental X2 Technology Demonstrator reached approximately 293 mph in 2010. The X2 used a coaxial rotor system combined with a pusher propeller at the tail to overcome the retreating blade stall that limits conventional helicopter speed. While not an FAI-certified record due to its experimental configuration, the X2 demonstrated that helicopters of the future could rival small airplanes in cruise performance.

Altitude Records

40,820 ft
Absolute Altitude Record

Aérospatiale SA 315B Lama — June 21, 1972

The absolute altitude record for a helicopter has stood since 1972. French test pilot Jean Boulet flew a specially prepared Aérospatiale Lama to 40,820 feet — over 7.7 miles above sea level. During the descent the engine flamed out due to extreme cold and thin air, and Boulet executed what may be the longest autorotation in history, gliding the unpowered helicopter back to earth safely.

29,029 ft
Everest Summit Landing

Eurocopter AS350 B3 — May 14, 2005

French pilot Didier Delsalle landed an Eurocopter AS350 B3 on the summit of Mount Everest on May 14, 2005 — the highest landing ever made by a helicopter. At 29,029 feet the air is so thin that even the modified AS350 was operating at the absolute edge of its capability. Delsalle landed, shut down, and restarted the engine on the summit before flying back down — then repeated the feat the following day to confirm the record.

Distance and Endurance Records

24,802 miles
First Solo Circumnavigation

Jennifer Murray — Robinson R44 — 1997

In 1997, Jennifer Murray became the first woman to fly solo around the world in a helicopter, completing the journey in a Robinson R44 — a light piston helicopter never designed for such an undertaking. The flight took 97 days and covered approximately 24,802 miles across 26 countries. Her achievement remains one of the most impressive long-distance helicopter flights in history.

Both Poles
First Helicopter Polar Circumnavigation

Jennifer Murray & Colin Bodill — 2007

Murray returned to the record books in 2007, flying with co-pilot Colin Bodill in a Bell 407 from the North Pole to the South Pole and back — the first helicopter circumnavigation via both poles. The journey required extraordinary navigation and the logistical challenge of refueling at some of the most remote locations on the planet.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the fastest helicopter in the world?

The fastest production helicopter currently in service is the Sikorsky S-97 Raider, which has reached speeds approaching 240 mph in testing. For certified FAI records, the Westland Lynx still holds the absolute speed record at 253 mph set in 1986.

Can a helicopter fly to the top of Everest?

Yes — it has been done. Didier Delsalle landed a specially prepared Eurocopter AS350 B3 on the Everest summit in 2005. It requires a high-performance aircraft operating near its absolute ceiling, favorable weather, and exceptional piloting skill.

How high can a normal helicopter fly?

Most light helicopters like the Robinson R44 have a certified service ceiling of around 14,000 feet. Turbine helicopters like the Airbus H125 are certified to 23,000 feet. Flying near these ceilings requires careful power management and awareness of high-altitude performance limitations.

Has a helicopter ever flown on another planet?

Yes. NASA's Ingenuity helicopter flew on Mars beginning in April 2021 — the first powered flight on another planet. Ingenuity uses a rotor system to generate lift in Mars's extremely thin atmosphere, making it one of the most remarkable aviation achievements in history.
