How to Become a Helicopter Pilot

To earn your Private Pilot Certificate (Helicopter), you need a minimum of 40 flight hours, including 20 hours with an instructor and 10 hours of solo flight. You'll also pass an FAA written knowledge test and a practical checkride. Most students complete their private certificate in 3–6 months at an accredited flight school. The Robinson R22 is the most common training helicopter due to its low operating cost. After your private, you can pursue a Commercial Certificate at 150 hours, opening the door to paid flying jobs.

💼

Helicopter Career Paths in the USA

The helicopter industry offers diverse career paths: EMS/Air Medical (flying for hospital networks), law enforcement aviation, offshore oil and gas transport, military aviation, tour operations (Hawaii, Grand Canyon, NYC), utility work (power line patrol, firefighting), and flight instruction. Entry-level positions typically require 500–1,500 total flight hours. EMS pilots typically need 2,000+ hours and an instrument rating. Salaries range from $50,000 for new CFIs to over $120,000 for experienced EMS pilots.

🗺️

Understanding Heliports in the USA

The FAA maintains a national database of over 5,000 registered heliports across all 50 states. Heliports are classified as public-use (open to all), private-use (restricted access), or hospital heliports. They range from rooftop pads in major cities to rural landing areas used by agricultural operators. Use our Heliports tab to search the FAA database by state, or search directly at the FAA's official Airport Data site (faa.gov).

🚁

Buying a Used Helicopter: What to Know

The used helicopter market offers options at many price points. A Robinson R22 typically starts around $60,000–$120,000. A Robinson R44 ranges from $100,000–$250,000. Turbine helicopters like the Bell 206 JetRanger start around $200,000–$500,000. Before buying, always have an independent A&P mechanic inspect the aircraft. Check total time on the airframe, time since major overhaul, and review the maintenance logbooks carefully. Hull insurance typically runs 2–4% of the aircraft's value annually.

Best Helicopter Tour Destinations in the USA

The Grand Canyon in Arizona offers breathtaking flights over one of the world's greatest natural wonders, with departures from Las Vegas and Grand Canyon Village. Hawaii offers tours over active volcanoes, waterfalls, and lush valleys on every island. New York City tours offer views of Manhattan, the Statue of Liberty, and the Brooklyn Bridge. Alaska glacier tours let you land on ancient ice. Tour prices range from $99 to over $400 per person depending on duration and destination.

🔧

Helicopter Maintenance & A&P Mechanics

Helicopter maintenance is performed by FAA-certificated Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) mechanics. Helicopters require more frequent maintenance than fixed-wing aircraft due to the complexity of the rotor system. Most Robinson helicopters have 100-hour inspection intervals and major overhauls at 2,200 hours. A&P mechanics specializing in rotorcraft are in high demand — salaries range from $60,000 to over $100,000 per year. Use our Jobs board to find current A&P mechanic openings across the USA.
